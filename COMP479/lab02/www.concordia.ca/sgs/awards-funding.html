AWARDS & FUNDING

How will you pay for tuition?

Concordia gives out over 1,700 awards totalling more than $15 million. These include generous scholarships from our donors, financial support for conference and travel.

For prospective students

Provincial & federal awards

The Quebec and Canadian governments offer a number of competitive graduate scholarships. We encourage you to apply for these awards at the same time you are preparing your application.

Learn more

Entrance awards

Students are automatically considered for all entrance graduate awards when they apply to Concordia, provided they meet eligibility criteria. No separate application is required.

Learn more

Special entrance award

Newly admitted students already benefiting from provincial, federal and prestigious foundation scholarships funding are offered a Special Entrance Award valued at $6,000 for master's and $10,000 for doctoral students.

Learn more

For current students

Provincial & federal awards

Numerous government agencies offer graduate scholarships and postdoctoral fellowships on an annual basis. These include the prestigious Trudeau and Vanier scholarships.

Learn more

Conference & exposition awards

Doctoral students can travel anywhere in the world and receive funding of up to $15,000 total while registered in their program. Master's students can benefit from conference funding too. 

Learn more

Standout research award

Valued at $1,000, this biannual competition awards exceptional research projects that have been released to the public in the eight months prior to the deadline date.

Learn more

Quebec Merit Scholarship for Foreign Nationals

The Quebec Merit (PBEEE) scholarship program for foreign nationals is open to international Doctoral and Postdoctoral students from any research sector.

Apply for the Quebec Merit

For postdocs

Banting

The Banting Postdoctoral Fellowships Program is administered by Canada's three federal granting agencies (SSHRC, NSERC, and CIHR) to attract and retain top-tier postdoctoral talent, both nationally and internationally, to contribute to Canada's economic, social and research-based growth.

Learn more

Horizon

Concordia funds up to 24 Horizon postdoctoral appointments per year in a range of disciplines where supervisors have demonstrated a link to our Strategic Research Plan. A call for applications is released based on projects selected in a highly competitive process.

Learn more

Funding sources

Awards from a variety of sources, including Concordia-funded opportunities, private donors, and the federal and provincial Governments, are available to international and domestic graduate students.

Consult the deadlines
